Madrid

Start your day right:
Brunch at Mazel Bagels (NYC-style bagels) or El Gordito (amazing mini sandwiches and mojitos) is a must. For lunch, Honest Greens is perfect — healthy, delicious, and super aesthetic.

Craving tacos?
Run (don’t walk) to Takos al Pastor. Best tacos in Madrid and super affordable — but there’s always a line, so get there early.

Dinner with a vibe:
Reserve in advance for Amazónico, Ten con Ten, or Bel Mondo. Think vibey, candlelit, and influencer-approved spots with amazing food.

Where to shop:
Gran Vía is the go-to for Zara, Mango, and all the big brands. But head to Malasaña or Chueca for thrift stores, edgy boutiques, and local fashion. Gran Vía x Fuencarral is also great for vintage.

Chill moments:
Take a boat ride at Retiro Park and visit the Crystal Palace for cute photo ops. Want culture? Check out the Prado Museum or Reina Sofía for Picasso’s Guernica.

Bars and rooftops:
Pre-game at Salmon Guru (one of the top bars in the world), then head to Riu Rooftop for insane sunset views. Macera is great for spicy drinks too.

Club time:
Start at Kapital (7 floors, crazy big), Toy Room (Wednesday frat vibes), or Istar (house music, favorite on Tuesdays). Madrid clubs go late — don’t show up before 1:30 AM.

Pro tip:
Madrid doesn’t sleep. Expect to eat at 10 PM and dance until 5 AM. The energy is unmatched and you’ll never want to leave.

London

Start your day right:
For a classic English breakfast, go to The Wolseley in Mayfair or Regency Café (you've definitely seen it on TikTok). If you want something trendy, check out Caravan in King's Cross or Granger & Co in Notting Hill.

Lunch & dinner spots:
Lunch at Padella (homemade pasta, affordable, and worth the line) or Flat Iron for steak under £15. For dinner, Dishoom is the top pick — get the black daal and house chaat. You can also try Sketch for a fancy vibe or Duck & Waffle for food with a view.

Markets & quick eats:
Explore Borough Market for food stalls, Camden Market for alternative eats and vintage clothes, and Spitalfields for trendy vendors and bites.

Shopping:
Hit Oxford Street for the big-name stores, but for thrifting and edgy fashion, go to Brick Lane, Shoreditch, and Notting Hill. For high-end, Selfridges, Liberty, and Harrods are iconic.

Culture & attractions:
Visit Big Ben, Tower Bridge, Westminster Abbey, and Buckingham Palace. Museum-wise, check out the British Museum, Tate Modern, and Victoria & Albert Museum. Most are free.

Rooftops & drinks:
Go to Sky Garden for panoramic views, Madison for a rooftop cocktail, or Tonight Josephine for pink neon and girl-dinner energy. 12th Knot and Sabine are also solid picks.

Nightlife:
Party at XOYO, Ministry of Sound, or Cirque Le Soir for wild club vibes. If you're into underground or house, Phonox and Fabric are top-tier. London goes late, so pace yourself.

Paris

Start your day right:
Grab a croissant at La Maison d’Isabelle (award-winning) and chill at Luxembourg Gardens. For a real brunch, go to Holybelly or Season — best brunch spots that locals actually go to.

Lunch & dinner:
Hit Breizh Café for the best crêpes or Café de Flore for a classic Parisian lunch. For dinner, book Girafe (for insane Eiffel Tower views), Benoit (Michelin-starred but not pretentious), or Pink Mamma (Italian, super vibey and all over TikTok).

Where to shop:
Thrift and shop in Le Marais — the best area for vintage stores, cute cafés, and boutique shopping. For designer, go to Galeries Lafayette and Printemps. The rooftop at Galeries Lafayette is free and has insane views.

Must-see spots:
Walk through Montmartre and see Sacré-Cœur. Visit Champs-Élysées and Arc de Triomphe but don't stay too long. Hit Louvre early (buy tickets ahead) or go to Musée de l'Orangerie for Monet's Water Lilies.

Photo ops:
Take photos at Trocadéro (best Eiffel Tower shot), Rue de l’Université (hidden street shot), Palais Royal (black and white columns), and along the Seine River for golden hour.

Bars and rooftops:
Pre-game at Le Perchoir Marais or Terrass'' Hotel Rooftop. If you want hidden speakeasies, check out Lavomatic (through a laundromat door) and Little Red Door (ranked one of the best bars globally).

Nightlife:
Start with pregame bars around Oberkampf or Canal Saint-Martin. Clubs you should actually go to: Pachamama (four floors, Latin house party vibe), Deflower (after-hours, house music), Medellin Paris (Latin club, reggaeton and bottle service crowd), Silencio (celebrity and artsy scene), and Les Bains (fashion week, it-girl energy). For underground techno, Rex Club is the move.

Pro tip:
Paris clubs are strict. You need to dress up. Get there around 1 AM, party until sunrise, and be ready to vibe. Paris nightlife hits different when you do it right.

Milan

Start your day right:
Get coffee and pastries at Marchesi 1824 (luxury bakery) or Panificio Pattini (local favorite). For brunch, hit Gelsomina Milano (super cute, TikTok-famous) or California Bakery for a casual American-style brunch.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Luini for iconic panzerotti or Miscusi for fresh handmade pasta. For dinner, book Gloria (Big Mamma Group, stunning décor, top-tier Italian food) or Giacomo Bistrot (old-money Italian energy). If you want to flex, go to Casa Cipriani.

Where to shop:
Walk the Quadrilatero della Moda (Via Montenapoleone, Via della Spiga) for luxury fashion. For trendier and vintage spots, go to Brera and Navigli District. Don’t miss 10 Corso Como — one of the most aesthetic stores in Milan.

Must-see spots:
Visit the Duomo di Milano (go up to the rooftop), shop at Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, relax at Parco Sempione, and walk through Castello Sforzesco for cute park and castle vibes.

Photo ops:
Get rooftop shots at the Duomo, aesthetic streets in Brera, sunset shots by the Navigli canals, and artsy content at Fondazione Prada.

Bars and rooftops:
Grab drinks at Terrazza Aperol (by the Duomo) or Ceresio 7 (rooftop with a pool and insane skyline views). For speakeasy cocktails, try Nottingham Forest or Mag Café in Navigli.

Nightlife:
Best clubs: Gaspédiòll (underground house, real Milan crowd), Apollo Club (retro TikTok-famous dinner+party spot), Armani Privé (boujee and exclusive), The Club Milano (wild student energy), and Plastic Milano (fashion/edgy scene). For bar hopping, Navigli is the move.

Pro tip:
Milan nightlife starts late. Pregame around 10-11 PM, show up to clubs after midnight. Dress codes are strict — no sneakers, bring your best fit if you want to get in.

Sydney

Start your day right:
Grab coffee and breakfast at Speedos Café (Bondi Beach, iconic healthy brunch) or Bills Sydney (known for their ricotta hotcakes). If you want something lowkey but amazing, Paramount Coffee Project in Surry Hills is a must.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Fishbowl (sushi bowls, super fresh) or Bondi’s Best (if you’re feeling seafood with a view). For dinner, go to Chin Chin (Southeast Asian, trendy TikTok hotspot) or Mr. Wong (elegant Cantonese spot, top-tier). If you want boujee waterfront vibes, book a table at Icebergs Dining Room and Bar.

Where to shop:
Vintage and indie shopping at Surry Hills and Newtown. High-end designers are at Queen Victoria Building (QVB) and Westfield Sydney downtown. Bondi has cute boutique shops if you're in a chill mood.

Must-see spots:
Walk the Bondi to Coogee Coastal Walk (best views ever), visit the Royal Botanic Gardens next to the Opera House, and ferry over to Manly Beach for a more local vibe. If you have time, hit Watsons Bay for sunsets.

Photo ops:
Photos at Bondi Icebergs Pool (famous ocean pool), the Opera House from Mrs Macquarie’s Chair viewpoint, Bronte Baths, and the colorful street art in Newtown.

Bars and rooftops:
Pre-drinks at The Glenmore (rooftop bar with Harbour Bridge views) or Opera Bar (right by the water). For hidden speakeasy vibes, go to Maybe Sammy (one of the top 50 bars in the world) or Employees Only Sydney.

Nightlife:
Best clubs: Ivy (massive rooftop pool parties and EDM nights), Marquee Sydney (Vegas vibes, big DJs), Home The Venue (for big bass and late nights). For something more local, bar hop around Darlinghurst and Surry Hills.

Pro tip:
Sydney nightlife starts earlier than Europe — hit bars around 8-9 PM and clubs around 11 PM. Dress code is chill by the beach, but upscale downtown clubs expect smart casual (no flip flops).

Split

Start your day right:
Breakfast at Kava Café (best coffee in Split, super cute vibe) or Brasserie on 7 (harborfront brunch spot — Instagram famous). If you want a lowkey local vibe, check out Kantun Paulina for a quick Croatian sandwich (čevapi roll).

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Bokeria Kitchen & Wine (Mediterranean food, TikTok-famous) or Fig Split (fresh and artsy menu, veggie-friendly). For dinner, reserve at Zinfandel Food & Wine Bar (romantic, cozy, unreal seafood) or Restaurant Dvor (insane seaside views).

Where to shop:
Walk around Diocletian’s Palace area — it’s full of local boutiques, handmade jewelry, and art galleries. For a real flea market vibe, visit Pazar Market right outside the palace walls.

Must-see spots:
Explore inside Diocletian’s Palace (ancient Roman emperor’s palace, looks like a movie set), chill at Bačvice Beach (closest city beach), hike up Marjan Hill for crazy panoramic views, and stroll the Riva Promenade (especially at sunset).

Photo ops:
Pictures inside the palace courtyards, sunset shots along the Riva, beach photos at Kasjuni Beach (less crowded, more clear water), and view shots from Marjan Hill Lookout.

Bars and rooftops:
Start with drinks at Teraca Vidilica (best sunset view bar), casual wine at Zinfandel, or rooftop vibes at Roof 68. After that, head to Fabrique Pub (huge, popular pregame spot).

Nightlife:
Best clubs: Central the Club (Split’s biggest club, DJs, crazy light shows), Vanilla Club (local DJs and open-air parties in summer), and Tropic Club (beach club vibes late night). For beach day-to-night parties, chill at Bačvice Beach bars (very casual and fun).

Pro tip:
Split nightlife moves fast — pregames are early around 9 PM, clubs fill up 11 PM-midnight. Summer season is wild, especially July and August, so book tables if you want prime spots.

Florence

Start your day right:
Grab a coffee and pastry at Ditta Artigianale (Florence's best specialty coffee shop) or Caffè Gilli (historic and aesthetic for photos). For brunch, hit Melaleuca (TikTok-famous Aussie-style brunch) or La Ménagère (beautiful flower café — insane vibes).

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at All’Antico Vinaio (viral paninis — worth the line) or Trattoria ZaZa (pasta and Tuscan classics). For dinner, book La Giostra (romantic, celeb-loved Italian restaurant) or Osteria Santo Spirito (cozy, famous for their gnocchi). If you want a cool trendy spot, try Golden View Firenze (views over the Ponte Vecchio).

Where to shop:
Hit Via de' Tornabuoni for luxury designer shops. For vintage and unique finds, explore the San Lorenzo Market and the Oltrarno District (especially for jewelry and leather goods).

Must-see spots:
Climb to the top of the Duomo (best city view), walk across the Ponte Vecchio, visit the Uffizi Gallery (if you want art) and see Michelangelo’s David at the Accademia Gallery. Wander around Piazza della Signoria for perfect daytime pics.

Photo ops:
Take golden hour photos at Piazzale Michelangelo (crazy skyline sunset), cute street shots around Borgo San Jacopo, and glam shots by the Arno River at night.

Bars and rooftops:
Start with drinks at SE·STO on Arno (rooftop bar with insane city views) or La Terrazza Rooftop Bar (right near Ponte Vecchio). For trendy cocktails, go to Mad Souls & Spirits or Manifattura for old-school Italian vibes.

Nightlife:
Florence nightlife is underrated: hit Space Club (huge, chaotic dance floor — American college kids meet locals), Tenax (for house/techno if you want a real DJ night), and Flo Lounge Bar (seasonal rooftop summer club — super Instagrammable). For pregame, do casual bar hopping in Piazza Santo Spirito.

Pro tip:
Florence clubs start earlier — 10-11 PM is already poppin’. Also, the city is super walkable — wear cute shoes you can ACTUALLY walk in because cobblestone streets are no joke.

Dublin

Start your day right:
Grab coffee and brunch at Queen of Tarts (TikTok viral bakery and brunch spot) or Two Boys Brew (modern café, fire brunch). If you want something quick, Vice Coffee Inc. is a cute pick.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Brother Hubbard (Middle Eastern fusion, super aesthetic) or Woollen Mills (casual Irish comfort food). For dinner, book Fade Street Social (vibey, tapas-style Irish plates) or Mr. Fox (if you want a slightly fancier but still young vibe). If you want a pub dinner, The Old Storehouse is super fun.

Where to shop:
Walk down Grafton Street for all the big shops and local boutiques. For vintage thrifting and cool indie shops, hit Temple Bar District (not just for drinking) and George’s Street Arcade.

Must-see spots:
Visit Trinity College and see the Book of Kells, chill at St. Stephen’s Green Park, and walk around Dublin Castle. Temple Bar is chaotic but worth seeing once. Also take a quick trip out to Howth (coastal village vibes) if you have time.

Photo ops:
Sunset shots along the Ha'penny Bridge, cute campus shots at Trinity College, pub pics in Temple Bar, and seaside photos if you make it to Howth Cliffs.

Bars and pubs:
Start your night at The Temple Bar Pub (yes it's touristy but fun), then go to The Palace Bar (super traditional Irish pub). If you want cocktails, go to Peruke & Periwig (super aesthetic, dark old-timey vibes) or The Vintage Cocktail Club (hidden speakeasy — book ahead).

Nightlife:
Dublin turns up. Best clubs: Dicey’s Garden (cheap drinks, outdoor club energy), Copper Face Jacks (chaotic Irish college crowd), and The George (huge fun LGBTQ+ nightclub, very welcoming and wild). If you want bar hopping, just start in Camden Street — it's nonstop energy.

Pro tip:
Drinks start EARLY in Dublin — people pregame around 7-8 PM, and bars/clubs pop off by 10-11 PM. It’s super casual — sneakers are totally fine unless you’re going to a fancier cocktail bar.

Prague

Start your day right:
Get brunch at Café Savoy (iconic, aesthetic, fancy af) or Můj šálek kávy (trendy coffee shop with fire brunch). If you want an easy quick one, Eska is a huge TikTok foodie spot — crazy good pastries and eggs.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Lokál (authentic Czech food, super cheap and TikTok viral) or Manifesto Market (open-air food market — crazy aesthetic and tons of options). For dinner, hit V Zátiší (nice Czech fusion) or go vibey at Kampa Park (boujee riverside dining). For a lowkey dinner, Sad Man’s Tongue Bar & Bistro is burgers + retro rock vibes.

Where to shop:
Wander the Pařížská Street (designer boutiques if you're feeling boujee). Otherwise hit Havelské Tržiště (cute market for handmade jewelry, souvenirs) or Vinohrady District for thrift and vintage finds.

Must-see spots:
Walk across the Charles Bridge (best at sunrise or sunset), visit the Old Town Square (famous clock tower and colorful buildings), see the Prague Castle and St. Vitus Cathedral (huge photo ops). Also check out Lennon Wall for street art.

Photo ops:
Charles Bridge at sunrise, rooftops overlooking Old Town, inside Prague Castle, and vibey riverwalk shots by Naplavka.

Bars and rooftops:
Start with drinks at T-Anker Rooftop Bar (insane panoramic views), Hemingway Bar (one of the top cocktail bars in the world — book ahead), and Anonymous Shrink’s Office (crazy hidden speakeasy experience). Also check out Cloud 9 Sky Bar for skyline views.

Nightlife:
PRAGUE GOES OFF. Clubs: Epic Prague (massive, EDM vibes, lasers everywhere), Roxy (underground, techno, and house — artsy crowd), and of course Lucerna Music Bar (giant throwback 80s/90s dance parties). If you want weird but iconic: Karlovy Lázně (5-floor club — every floor is a different music vibe, touristy but insane).

Pro tip:
Prague nightlife is CHEAP — beers are literally $2-3. Pregame hard because drinks are cheap at bars but club bottles still cost $$$. Ubers are super cheap so hop around easily.

Lisbon

Start your day right:
Start with brunch at Heim Café (trendy, aesthetic, avocado toast heaven) or Nicolau Lisboa (TikTok viral — super cute interior and unreal pancakes). If you want somewhere even more chill, try Dear Breakfast (modern brunch staple).

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Time Out Market (TikTok-famous food market — tons of stands from top chefs) or O Botanista (cool vegan/flexitarian if you want something lighter). For dinner, book Taberna da Rua das Flores (insane traditional Portuguese food) or Pharmacia (restaurant inside an old pharmacy — vibey, patio seating). If you want trendy boujee dinner: Seen Lisboa (rooftop restaurant, crazy city views and cocktails).

Where to shop:
Explore Avenida da Liberdade (luxury brands) or wander the Bairro Alto district for vintage shops, Portuguese boutiques, and unique ceramics. The LX Factory (TikTok-famous) is a must: old factory turned into boutiques, bookstores, cafes, rooftop bars.

Must-see spots:
Visit the Belém Tower and eat a real Pastel de Nata (custard tart) from Pastéis de Belém. Walk through Alfama (old town, tiny streets, best pics), ride the famous Tram 28 for classic Lisbon vibes, and head up to the Miradouro da Senhora do Monte for sunset views.

Photo ops:
Sunset at Miradouro de Santa Luzia, pics along the colorful Alfama alleys, tram street shots, the pink street (Rua Nova do Carvalho) for nighttime content, and city views from Miradouro da Graça.

Bars and rooftops:
Start with rooftop drinks at Park Bar (hidden on top of a parking garage, best sunset vibe), Topo Martim Moniz (rooftop cocktails with castle views), or Sky Bar by SEEN (super boujee, right at dinner spot Seen). Also hit Pensão Amor (old brothel turned cocktail bar — very TikTok famous).

Nightlife:
Lisbon goes HARD. Club at Lust in Rio (open-air, crazy parties), Plateau (throwback vibes, very fun), and Musicbox (underground electronic music — very local). Pregame in Bairro Alto — the whole neighborhood is literally one giant street party every night.

Pro tip:
Ubers are insanely cheap here, like €3–5. Wear sneakers — Lisbon streets are steep and cobblestoned AF. Book dinner and club tables early if you’re coming during summer weekends — Lisbon is blowing up on TikTok.

Amsterdam

Start your day right:
Hit MOAK Pancakes (TikTok-viral for their over-the-top pancake stacks) or Corner Bakery (super aesthetic and Instagrammable). If you want something more relaxed, go to Pluk or Oeuf — both are brunch goals.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Foodhallen (indoor food market with every kind of cuisine and cocktails) or Broodje Bert (casual but iconic sandwiches). Dinner at De Kas (high-end, greenhouse farm-to-table experience) or Gloria Amsterdam (super vibey with a curated seasonal menu). For canal-side dining, try Café de Jaren or Venus & Adonis (romantic, candle-lit, right on the water).

Where to shop:
Explore the Nine Streets (De 9 Straatjes) — boutique shopping heaven with vintage, designer, and concept stores. For thrifting and local finds, go to Episode or Waterlooplein Market.

Must-see spots:
Walk or bike through Jordaan (cutest neighborhood ever), cruise the Prinsengracht canal, and visit Anne Frank House (book tix early). Picnic in Vondelpark, see the tulips at Bloemenmarkt, and check out Moco Museum (Banksy + modern art — very TikTok friendly).

Photo ops:
Take golden hour pics on Magere Brug (the skinny bridge), shoot outside Rijksmuseum or on the Brouwersgracht canal, and rent a paddle boat or bike for content by the water. Also get snaps at A’DAM Lookout (glass swing over the city).

Bars and rooftops:
Go to SkyLounge Amsterdam (best city views), Canvas (chill rooftop with sunset drinks), and W Lounge Rooftop (poolside energy). For cocktails, try Flying Dutchmen or Tales & Spirits — both insane drink menus.

Nightlife:
Start the night in Leidseplein or Rembrandtplein for classic bar hopping. Then hit De School (legendary techno club in a former school — super underground vibes), Shelter (EDM/house — insane sound system), or Club NYX (queer-friendly, chaotic, 3 floors of pure energy). If you want something rowdy and touristy, there’s always Escape — but it’s giving frat vibes.

Pro tip:
Rent a bike (you’ll feel like a local) but be careful — Dutch bikers are serious. Ubers are rare, so walk or tram when you’re tired. Most clubs stay open until 5–6 AM — don’t show up before midnight. Book restaurants early — the good spots fill FAST.

Athens

Start your day right:
Brunch at Ohh Boy (super cute interior, matcha, Greek-fusion brunch), ERGON House (brunch inside a gourmet market — real aesthetic), or Dope Roasting Co. (minimalist, fire coffee + pastries). Another vibey favorite: Morning Bar — great playlist, plant-filled café energy.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Kostas Souvlaki (cheap, iconic souvlaki stand — TikTok-famous), Avocado (Mediterranean + vegan options), or Karamanlidika tou Fani (meze plates + charcuterie fire). For dinner, try Six D.O.G.S. (hidden garden bar + dinner), or book Hytra for rooftop dining with Acropolis views.

Where to shop:
Wander through Ermou Street (main shopping strip), but for real finds head to Kallidromiou Street Market on Saturdays or explore Exarchia and Psiri neighborhoods for vintage stores, concept shops, and artsy books/crafts.

Must-see spots:
Obviously see the Acropolis + Parthenon (go early to avoid crowds). Visit Temple of Hephaestus, explore Plaka (pastel streets and stairs everywhere), and chill at National Garden of Athens. If you want a beach break, hit Vouliagmeni Lake or Astir Beach.

Photo ops:
Sunset at Lycabettus Hill (views of whole city), rooftop drinks with the Acropolis lit up, Plaka’s blue doors + pastel walls, and edgy graffiti shots in Exarchia.

Bars and rooftops:
Drinks at A for Athens (the most classic rooftop bar with direct Acropolis view), Couleur Locale (sunset vibes, lit crowd), and The Zillers (super sleek rooftop). Want more alternative vibes? Go to Baba au Rum (cocktail heaven) or Cantina Social (underground grungy courtyard bar).

Nightlife:
Athens doesn’t sleep. Start the night at Six D.O.G.S. or Clumsies (world-famous cocktail bar). Then go to Lohan Nightclub (co-owned by Lindsay Lohan, no joke), Bolivar Beach Bar (summer club ON the beach), or Steam Athens (techno warehouse). You’ll find something every night of the week — clubs pop off past midnight and stay open until 6am.

Pro tip:
Uber doesn’t work here — use the local app Beat. Taxis are cheap. Always have cash — not everywhere takes card. Hit the ruins in the morning, beach in the afternoon, and rooftop/club at night. Trust: Athens is WAY more than just ancient stones.

Morocco

Start your day right:
Brunch at Plus61 (Australian-Moroccan fusion, TikTok fav), La Famille (hidden garden café with plant-based dishes), or Bacha Coffee (a literal palace café — like sipping espresso in a dream). All aesthetic, all brunch-girl-approved.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Café Des Épices (chill rooftop in the middle of the souks), or Nomad (modern Moroccan, great views and lighting for pics). Dinner at Le Jardin (green courtyard escape), Comptoir Darna (classic dinner + belly dancing performances), or Dar Yacout (super boujee set menu in a palace — pure ✨drama✨).

Where to shop:
Get lost in the Medina souks: rugs, lanterns, spices, handmade bags — it’s chaos in the best way. Go to Max & Jan for modern Moroccan fashion, Chabi Chic for ceramics, and 33 Rue Majorelle for luxury concept store vibes.

Must-see spots:
Visit Jardin Majorelle (blue villa + cacti, most IG-famous spot in the city), Bahia Palace, and Koutoubia Mosque. Take a camel ride or quad bike trip in the Agafay Desert, or just book a day at a fancy riad spa with a traditional hammam. Also visit Ben Youssef Madrasa for stunning tile work + courtyard pics.

Photo ops:
Every rooftop café, Jardin Majorelle’s blue wall, souks full of color, and the riad courtyards with tile/mosaic floors. Best light = golden hour in the Medina or desert sunset shoot.

Bars and rooftops:
Not a drinking-heavy city, but still options. Try Sky Lounge at The Pearl Hotel or Kabana Rooftop (trendy, cocktails, DJ sets). Also Le Salama is stunning with old-Hollywood Moroccan energy. If you want low-key: Barometre does craft cocktails in a speakeasy setting.

Nightlife:
Marrakech goes late. Theatro is THE club — over-the-top shows and music, super glam. Also check 555 Famous Club for DJs and dancing. Pre-game with dinner + live music at Comptoir Darna, then go out. Clubs get going after 12am. Fashion = full send.

Pro tip:
Always barter in the souks — start at 1/3 the price. Dress modest but stylish (long dresses, loose pants, crop tops with jackets). Bring cash. Some places won’t take card. Book desert excursions or hammam treatments ahead — TikTok made these book up fast.

Rome

Start your day right:
Brunch isn’t big in Italy but do a soft launch at Marigold (Scandi-minimalist café, TikTok viral), or grab pistachio cornettos at Roscioli Caffè (Italian-style standing coffee bar). Or for a sit-down: Bakery House has a full brunch menu if you’re really craving eggs + avo.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Forno Campo de' Fiori for pizza by the slice. Dinner at Osteria da Fortunata (fresh pasta hand-rolled in front of you, huge TikTok fame), Trattoria Al Moro (hidden classic, carbonara masterclass), or Gloria Trastevere for vibes + cocktails. You want aesthetic AND real Roman flavor? Do Rimessa Roscioli or Antico Arco for boujee.

Where to shop:
Vintage at Humana Vintage, Pifebo, or head to Via del Governo Vecchio. For modern shopping, hit Via del Corso or the boutiques in Monti.

Must-see spots:
Obviously Colosseum, Trevi Fountain, Pantheon, and Vatican Museums. But also: Giardino degli Aranci (orange garden with unreal view), Villa Borghese for sunset picnic, and Aventine Keyhole (secret view of St. Peter’s).

Photo ops:
Trevi at sunrise, Spanish Steps, Trastevere alleys, rooftop at Zuma Rome, and sunset from Pincian Hill.

Nightlife:
Pre-dinner spritz at Il Goccetto. Then go to Jerry Thomas Speakeasy (book ahead), Drink Kong (ranked in world’s top 50 bars), and clubs like Shari Vari or Gaspirillo for late-night dancing. Want chill rooftop: Terrazza Borromini or Cielo Terrace.

Melbourne

Start your day right:
Go to Higher Ground, Top Paddock, or The Kettle Black — Melbourne brunch is ELITE. Matcha, avo toast, ricotta hotcakes — all content-worthy. Coffee is religion here — get a flat white at Axil or Industry Beans.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Hakata Gensuke Ramen or Hardware Société (French-Aussie fusion). Dinner at Chin Chin (southeast Asian + club energy), Supernormal (modern Japanese), or Embla (natural wine + Euro plates). Also check Lucy Liu for vibey modern Asian.

Where to shop:
Thrift in Fitzroy, shop designer on Collins Street, and hit Emporium Melbourne for everything else. Pro tip: Queen Victoria Market on Sundays = vibes + vintage.

Must-see spots:
Street art in Hosier Lane, coastal drive to Brighton Beach Boxes, rooftop walk at Skydeck, and catch golden hour at St Kilda Pier. If you have time: take a train to Great Ocean Road for dramatic cliffs + pics.

Photo ops:
Hotcakes at Higher Ground, colored beach huts, Fitzroy murals, skyline shots from Naked for Satan Rooftop.

Nightlife:
Start at Ferdydurke or Section 8 (alleyway bars). For clubbing: Revolver Upstairs (iconic), Tramp Bar (underground vibes), or Yah Yah’s (live music + dancing).

Copenhagen

Start your day right:
Brunch at Mad & Kaffe (custom plates, super aesthetic), The Union Kitchen, or Atelier September (matcha and minimalist heaven).

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Gasoline Grill (burgers in a gas station), Torvehallerne Market for smørrebrød and fresh food stalls. Dinner at POPL (by the Noma team), Barr, or go high-end at Geranium (if you're rich af).

Where to shop:
Strøget for main brands, Nørrebro for vintage and artsy shops. Also: Wood Wood, Ganni, and Studio Arhoj for Scandi core.

Must-see spots:
Nyhavn (colorful canal), Rosenborg Castle, Tivoli Gardens (oldest amusement park), and Superkilen Park. Take a canal cruise or visit Christianshavn for a local vibe.

Photo ops:
Nyhavn, mirror glass at Louisiana Museum, marble steps at Frederik's Church, and the staircase inside Hotel Ottilia Rooftop.

Nightlife:
Drinks at The Jane (hidden speakeasy), Balderdash (quirky cocktails), or hit Culture Box for techno. Pre-game with wine at Pompette or Ved Stranden 10.

Tokyo

Start your day right:
Brunch at Aoyama Flower Market Tea House (flower café with tea sets), Path (croissants + eggs in Shibuya), or Franze & Evans (UK-inspired, aesthetic AF).

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Ichiran Ramen or Uobei (sushi train — TikTok fav). Dinner at Gyukatsu Motomura (beef cutlet), Narisawa (Michelin if you're boujee), or Kyubey for traditional omakase.

Where to shop:
Harajuku for streetwear and chaos, Shibuya 109 for Y2K, Daikanyama for upscale boutiques. Vintage? Go to Kinji or Chicago Omotesando.

Must-see spots:
Shibuya Crossing, Meiji Shrine, Asakusa Senso-ji, TeamLab Planets (immersive art), and Tokyo Tower.

Photo ops:
Shibuya rooftop, Harajuku alley shots, teamLab, arcades in Akihabara, and sunset from Shibuya Sky.

Nightlife:
Golden Gai (tiny bar alleys), Bar Benfiddich (craft cocktails), or Womb and ageHa (clubs that go til sunrise). Also try Gen Yamamoto for an elegant 6-drink experience.

Stockholm

Start your day right:
Brunch at Pom & Flora (IG-famous), Greasy Spoon (British-style brunch), or Brunchoteket. Fika (coffee + pastry) is a MUST — go to Vete-Katten or Café Pascal.

Lunch & dinner:
Lunch at Meatballs for the People (duh), or Teatern Food Court for Nordic street food. Dinner at Riche (buzzy bistro), Pelikan (old-school Swedish), or Tak (pan-Asian rooftop, great for sunset).

Where to shop:
Shop on Drottninggatan and Södermalm for thrift, Ganni, & Other Stories, and Acne Studios. Also check Grandpa for local design items.

Must-see spots:
Gamla Stan (old town), Vasa Museum, ABBA Museum, Fotografiska, and Skansen. Take a boat ride through the archipelago.

Photo ops:
Gamla Stan’s cobblestone streets, Monteliusvägen for skyline views, and Stortorget Square.

Nightlife:
Start at Tweed or Mälarpaviljongen, then head to Trädgården (open-air summer club under a bridge), Berns (boujee club + venue), or Under Bron (techno/alt crowd).
